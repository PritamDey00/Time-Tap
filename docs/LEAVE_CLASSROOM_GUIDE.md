## Overview

The Leave Classroom feature allows users to exit custom classrooms when they no longer wish to participate. This feature provides a clean way to manage classroom membership and ensures leaderboards only show active participants.

## üéØ What the Leave Classroom Feature Does

### ‚úÖ When You Leave a Classroom:
- **Removes You from Classroom**: You are no longer a member of that specific classroom
- **Updates Leaderboards**: Your name is removed from that classroom's leaderboard
- **Clears Participation**: You won't receive classroom-specific notifications or updates
- **Maintains Progress**: Your overall points and streak in the Universal Classroom remain unchanged

### ‚ùå What Doesn't Change:
- **Universal Classroom**: You remain in the main Universal Classroom
- **Overall Progress**: Your total points, streak, and account remain intact
- **Other Classrooms**: Membership in other custom classrooms is unaffected
- **Account Status**: Your user account and login remain active

## üöÄ How to Leave a Classroom

### Step-by-Step Process:

#### 1. Navigate to the Classroom:
- Go to the specific custom classroom you want to leave
- Ensure you're in the classroom page (not the classroom list)

#### 2. Access Classroom Settings:
- Look for the "Settings" button or gear icon in the classroom interface
- Click on "Settings" to open the classroom settings panel

#### 3. Find Leave Option:
- In the settings panel, locate the "Leave Classroom" button
- The button is typically prominently displayed and may be colored differently (often red)

#### 4. Confirm Your Decision:
- Click the "Leave Classroom" button
- A confirmation dialog may appear asking if you're sure
- Confirm your decision to proceed with leaving

#### 5. Automatic Redirect:
- After leaving, you'll be automatically redirected to the classroom list page
- You'll see a confirmation that you've successfully left the classroom

### Visual Indicators:
- **Settings Button**: Usually a gear icon (‚öôÔ∏è) or "Settings" text
- **Leave Button**: Often red or prominently colored for visibility

## üé≠ What Happens After Leaving

### Immediate Effects:
- **Leaderboard Removal**: Your name disappears from that classroom's leaderboard immediately
- **Membership Update**: The classroom's member count decreases by one
- **Access Removal**: You can no longer access that specific classroom's content
- **Notification Stop**: You stop receiving notifications specific to that classroom

### Long-term Effects:
- **Re-joining**: You can rejoin the same classroom later if it's still available
- **Progress Reset**: If you rejoin, you may start fresh in that classroom's context
- **History**: Your participation history in that classroom may be cleared
- **Relationships**: Any classroom-specific connections or progress are reset

## üîß Technical Details

### How It Works:
- **Server Update**: Your user ID is removed from the classroom's member list
- **Database Sync**: All classroom-related data is updated immediately
- **Real-time Updates**: Other users see the membership change in real-time
- **Clean Removal**: All traces of your participation in that classroom are removed

### Data Handling:
- **Personal Data**: Your overall account data remains unchanged
- **Classroom Data**: Your classroom-specific participation data is removed
- **Leaderboard Data**: Your entries in that classroom's leaderboard are deleted
- **Chat History**: Your chat messages in that classroom may remain but are no longer associated with active membership

## üéØ Use Cases

### When to Leave a Classroom:

#### Academic Reasons:
- **Course Completion**: Finished the course or subject
- **Schedule Change**: No longer taking that class
- **Semester End**: Academic term has ended
- **Transfer**: Moving to a different section or class

#### Personal Reasons:
- **Time Management**: Need to focus on fewer classrooms
- **Interest Change**: No longer interested in the subject
- **Conflict Resolution**: Avoiding conflicts with other participants
- **Privacy**: Want to limit your classroom participation

#### Technical Reasons:
- **Performance**: Too many classrooms affecting app performance
- **Notification Overload**: Reducing notification volume
- **Organization**: Cleaning up your classroom list
- **Testing**: Leaving test or experimental classrooms

### When NOT to Leave:
- **Temporary Issues**: Short-term problems that might resolve
- **Instructor Requirements**: When participation is mandatory
- **Group Projects**: When you're part of ongoing collaborative work
- **Assessment Periods**: During active grading or evaluation periods

## üõ†Ô∏è Troubleshooting

### Common Issues:

#### Can't Find Leave Button:
- **Check Settings**: Ensure you're in the classroom settings panel
- **Permissions**: Verify you have permission to leave (some classrooms may restrict leaving)
- **Refresh Page**: Try refreshing the browser page
- **Different Browser**: Test in a different browser

#### Leave Button Not Working:
- **Network Connection**: Check your internet connection
- **Server Issues**: The server might be temporarily unavailable
- **Browser Cache**: Clear browser cache and cookies
- **JavaScript Errors**: Check browser console for error messages (F12 ‚Üí Console)

#### Still Showing in Leaderboard:
- **Wait for Update**: Leaderboards may take a few moments to refresh
- **Refresh Page**: Manually refresh the classroom page
- **Cache Issues**: Clear browser cache to force data refresh
- **Server Sync**: The server might be processing the change

#### Can't Rejoin Classroom:
- **Classroom Status**: The classroom might be private or closed
- **Invitation Required**: You might need a new invitation to rejoin
- **Cooldown Period**: Some classrooms have waiting periods before rejoining
- **Permission Changes**: Classroom settings might have changed

### Getting Help:
If you're having trouble leaving a classroom:
1. **Contact Instructor**: Reach out to the classroom instructor or administrator
2. **Check Documentation**: Review any classroom-specific rules about leaving
3. **Technical Support**: Contact technical support with error details
4. **Alternative Methods**: Ask if there are alternative ways to leave the classroom
